What is justify-self?

The justify-self property controls the horizontal alignment of an element within its grid cell. In the demonstration above, you can see three coffee boxes positioned differently within their cells. The first box uses justify-self: start to align to the left edge, the middle box uses justify-self: center to center horizontally, and the last box uses justify-self: end to align to the right edge. This property only affects the individual item, not the entire grid. Think of it like choosing where to place your coffee cup on a serving tray - left side, center, or right side.

What is align-self?

The align-self property controls the vertical alignment of an element within its grid cell. In the demonstration above, you can see three coffee boxes positioned at different vertical positions. The first box uses align-self: start to align to the top, the middle box uses align-self: center to center vertically, and the last box uses align-self: end to align to the bottom of its cell. This is particularly useful when grid cells have different heights. Imagine stacking coffee cups on shelves - you can choose whether to place them at the top shelf, middle shelf, or bottom shelf. This property gives you precise vertical control over individual items in your grid layout.
